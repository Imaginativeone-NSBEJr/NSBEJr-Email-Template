Greetings!

Howard County NSBE Jr Supporters,

As we embark upon the 2020 - 2021 school year we are faced with a new set of opportunities designed to support our youth. With students scheduled to attend virtual classes, we are creating a wider variety of programs that keep students connected.


After successfully executing the First Annual Howard County Heart Challenge (March 2020), the chapter was forced to move all activities to a virtual platform.


The students of our chapter seized this opportunity to create the online talk show entitled “Pandora’s Box” (recordings soon to be available on YouTube). The talk show, designed to provide information “destined to change the world”, currently has 9 episodes with new episodes every two weeks.


Below are examples of regular segments from previous programs.

  • Good News Updates - positive stories of group or individual achievements
  • Financial Fitness - how and why to save; avoiding credit cards
  • Black History Trivia - team competitions awarding gift cards to the top three teams
  • Critics’ Choice - discussing “student friendly” tv series and movies

This Fall, students are eager to defend their 2nd and 3rd Place National Ranking in the NSBE Jr. National Math Competitions and continue with the TEN80 Racing Challenge Club (both newly designed to compete remotely).


To expand students learning opportunities, we are looking to add several new programs.

  1. Computer Learning Competitions - autonomous racing
    1. Deep Racer
    2. Drones
  2. Coding Competitions - Arduino based
  3. Website Development and Design
(All programs will be supported by Technology Professionals and the UMBC NSBE college students)


In an effort to continue providing outstanding programming we are looking for donations to support the chapter and its members.


Chapter Facts

NSBE Jr is a “Pre-College Initiative” (PCI), created by NSBE (National Society of Black Engineers, www.nsbe.org), to promote academic excellence, technology awareness, and leadership to all students in grades 5-12, regardless of nationality, race, religion or gender. Students participate in programming designed to promote an interest in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).


The Howard County NSBE Jr Chapter (HoCo NSBE Jr)/Next Generation Leadership Academy (NGLA) is a non-profit organization through a partnership with LEAPForward (Southern Maryland NSBE Jr Chapter) and provides targeted programming to students in grades 5-12, with a focus on “Personal Development”, “Career Planning” and “College Placement”.


Currently, HoCo NSBE Jr Chapter (hoconsbejr.com) has 85 members with 46 members achieving honor roll status.
